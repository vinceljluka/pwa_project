Europe, Prey to the Vampires of Populism

Across the continent, populist forces feed on disillusionment, polarization, and fear — slowly draining the lifeblood from democratic institutions.

Published on May 8, 2019 at 11:45

“They promise everything. They deliver division,”

warns political analyst Sofia Laurent. From Hungary to Italy, populist leaders paint themselves as champions of the people while undermining the press, courts, and minority rights.

Anti-elite rhetoric, fueled by social media and distrust in traditional institutions, has become a powerful weapon. Fear of immigration and economic decline serves as fertile ground.

In Brussels, there’s growing concern over the erosion of shared democratic values. Nationalist factions often block EU-wide agreements, further weakening unity.

The question facing Europe is no longer whether populism is rising — but whether liberal democracy can withstand its bite.
