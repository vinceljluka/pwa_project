Nancy: A Bright Outlook for Real Estate

The real estate market in Nancy continues to perform well. Low property prices and attractive loan rates allow buyers to access quality homes without sacrificing their purchasing power.

Published on May 16, 2019 at 07:00

“There should be queues forming in front of the banks,”

says Eric Junger, broker and deputy director at Crédit Expert, highlighting the strength of the Nancy market. This view is echoed by real estate professionals, who have seen steady transaction growth since 2016 — "a sign of a healthy and dynamic market," explains Damien Gégout, notary and member of the Real Estate Observatory of Meurthe-et-Moselle.

Loan rates "remain at historically low levels,” which attracts many young buyers. First-time buyers now represent more than a third of all transactions. “Today, a tenant can buy a similar property to the one they rent, with monthly payments only slightly higher than their rent. It’s a huge win for quality of life,” says Grégory Pierre, director of Century 21 in Nancy.

But buyers are not compromising on their standards, notes Stéphanie Delavaux, director of ADIL 54.

Proximity to work, quality finishes, space, and outdoor areas remain essential factors. Additionally, “Concerns like security or co-ownership issues can block a sale, because this market is not under pressure,” adds Grég.
