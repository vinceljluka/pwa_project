In the Meuse and Vosges: A Gloomy Property Landscape

Unlike other parts of France, the Meuse and Vosges struggle with declining property values, vacant homes, and fading economic appeal.

Published on May 21, 2019 at 10:10

“We see more houses empty than occupied in some towns,”

says local agent Marie Thibault. Depopulation and job losses have turned once-bustling villages into ghost-like spaces. The lack of demand has made real estate practically stagnant.

Many young people leave for bigger cities, leaving behind an aging population. As a result, prices drop — but so does investment.

Local governments attempt to revive interest through renovation grants and rural incentives, but recovery is slow.

Experts argue that without stronger infrastructure and employment prospects, the Meuse and Vosges may remain locked in decline.
