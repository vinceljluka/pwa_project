“Europeans Are Not the Problem”: In Austria, a Normalized Far Right

The rise of far-right politics in Austria is no longer a shock — it's part of the political landscape. Nationalist rhetoric is becoming mainstream.

Published on April 3, 2019 at 10:15

“We are not extremists. We are realists,”

said a representative of Austria's Freedom Party (FPÖ), now part of a coalition government. The party advocates strict immigration limits and prioritization of “native Austrians” in social programs.

Recent elections saw record turnout from rural voters, energized by concerns over cultural identity and economic uncertainty. Many now see the FPÖ as a legitimate force, not a fringe threat.

Human rights groups warn that this normalization risks eroding democratic institutions and fueling xenophobia. Still, polls show strong support for policies once considered too radical.

As Europe grapples with migration and identity politics, Austria’s experience offers a glimpse into how far-right ideas gain lasting political power.
