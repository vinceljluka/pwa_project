Christian Culture, Security, Family: These Are the Values Hungary Wants to Protect

Hungary has taken a firm stance on preserving traditional values as the cornerstone of its national identity — prioritizing religion, order, and family stability.

Published on March 12, 2019 at 09:30

“We are the defenders of Christian Europe,”

declared Prime Minister Viktor Orbán during a national address, reinforcing his government's commitment to a conservative cultural framework. Hungary, he insists, must resist what he calls "liberal decay" coming from Western Europe.

Policies introduced in recent years include financial incentives for families, strict immigration controls, and education reforms centered on national heritage and faith.

Critics argue that these measures marginalize minority groups and silence opposition voices. However, Orbán’s supporters claim the approach is necessary to maintain Hungary’s sovereignty and cultural survival.

International observers remain divided. Some view Hungary as a beacon of tradition in a rapidly shifting Europe; others see its government as increasingly authoritarian.
